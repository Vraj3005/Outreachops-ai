# OutreachOps AI v2 — User & Workflow Guide

This guide details the step-by-step workflow to configure settings, build campaigns, import files, personalize drafts, dispatch emails, and track outcomes.

---

## 1. Connect Integrations
1. Go to the **Integrations** section in the sidebar.
2. **Gmail connection**: Click **Link Gmail Account**. This triggers the Google OAuth 2.0 consent page. Grant the requested read/write/send permissions. Upon redirect, the backend secures the refresh and access tokens.
3. **Google Sheets connection**: Set your Sheets credentials by uploading the service account JSON or setting `SHEETS_CREDENTIALS_B64` in the backend environment.

---

## 2. Configure Settings
1. Navigate to the **Settings** view in the sidebar.
2. **Business profile**: Configure Business Name, Website, Timezone, Sender Name, and Signature.
3. **Outbox spacing & limits**: Set Daily Send Limit (e.g. 50 emails) and Send Spacing Interval (e.g. 60 seconds) to prevent spam flags.
4. **Banned phrases**: Populate phrases that should trigger warnings if generated by the AI model.

---

## 3. Import Leads & Map Columns
1. Navigate to **Imports** in the sidebar.
2. Select **Upload spreadsheet File** or enter a Google Sheet URL.
3. Once parsed, the **Column Mapping Wizard** will display.
4. Map the columns from your sheet (e.g. `Client Email`, `Business`) to the required lead fields (`contact_email`, `company_name`, `website`).
5. Click **Preview Mappings** to verify sample values, then click **Commit Import** to insert leads into the database.

---

## 4. Create Campaign
1. Go to the **Campaigns** section and click **New Campaign**.
2. **Campaign details**: Fill in Name, Target Industry, Objective (e.g., website conversion vs. service pitches), Tone, and CTA.
3. **Define Sequence Steps**: Add steps (e.g., Step 1, Step 2 Follow-up) and set delay metrics (in hours or days).
4. Enroll the newly imported leads into the campaign.

---

## 5. Tune & Test Prompts
1. Go to **Prompt Studio**.
2. Customize the system instructions and user prompt templates.
3. Click **Test Prompt Template** against a mock lead.
4. View the generated sample email along with its metrics (personalization score, spam risk, clarity index). If satisfactory, click **Save Version** and mark the prompt version as **Active**.

---

## 6. Generate Samples & Review
1. Navigate to the **Campaigns** details page.
2. Select 3-5 leads and click **Generate Sample Drafts**.
3. Go to the **Draft review queue** page.
4. Review the generated copy. If any edits are needed, edit the text block directly or click **Regenerate Draft** to trigger a new AI generation with updated guidance.
5. Click **Approve** on drafts that are ready for dispatch.

---

## 7. Run Batch Generation & Schedule
1. Once you approve the style samples, select all remaining leads in the campaign and click **Generate Batch**.
2. The background `GenerationWorker` will pick up the items, process them, and populate the draft outbox queue.
3. Review and **Approve** the batch drafts.
4. Approved drafts are enqueued as `pending` status inside `scheduled_emails` and scheduled according to the campaign spacing rules.

---

## 8. Monitor Outbox Dispatch
1. Head to the **Queue & Diagnostics** dashboard page.
2. The grid displays enqueued dispatches, schedules, and attempts.
3. The right-hand panel shows the running heartbeats of backend daemons.
4. If a send failure occurs due to transient network issues, the scheduler applies exponential backoff delays and marks it for retry.

---

## 9. Review Replies & Analyze Metrics
1. The **Gmail Reply Sync** worker polls your inbox.
2. When a reply is detected, the campaign sequence transitions to `stopped` for that lead.
3. Visit the **Analytics** dashboard to analyze campaign metrics: delivery rates, open rates, reply rates, and total replies received.
